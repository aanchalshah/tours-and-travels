Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument built in the early twentieth century in the city of Mumbai, in the Indian state of Maharashtra.

It was erected to commemorate the landing in December 1911 at Apollo Bunder, Mumbai (then Bombay) of King-Emperor George V and Queen-Empress Mary.

The gateway has also been referred to as the Taj Mahal of Mumbai, and is the city's top tourist attraction.
